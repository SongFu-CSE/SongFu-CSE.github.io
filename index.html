Dr. Song Fu is a Professor of Computer Science and Engineering with the University of North Texas. He is the Director of the NSF eCAT Center for Electric, Connected and Autonomous Technologies for Mobility, UNT Site. He also directs the Vehicle and Edge Computing Lab (VEC). His research interest is primarily in vehicle computing, IoT-edge-cloud computing, parallel and distributed systems, including architecture, performance, dependability, and machine learning. His research has been supported in part by funding from the National Science Foundation, Department of Defense, Department of Energy, National Laboratories, Amazon, Cisco, Fujitsu, Google, Nvidia, PACCAR, Toyota, Xilinx, etc. He is an ACM Distinguished Member.

Dr. Fu currently serves as the Associate Chair for Research. He has been a Faculty Leadership Fellow (2021-2022) and an Academic Affairs Fellow (2023-2024), both of which are leadership programs organized by the UNT Office of the Provost.

Research Interests

Dr. Fu’s research is dedicated to the investigation, establishment, and experimental evaluation of new theoretical foundations and system artifacts to significantly improve the performance, scalability, security, and energy efficiency of computer systems and deep learning applications. His research interest is primarily in cyberinfrastructures, vehicles, edge-cloud systems, and deep learning technologies, including

 

  • Connected and Autonomous Vehicles, Vehicle Computing
  • Edge and Cloud Computing, Distributed Systems
  • Quantum Computing and Systems
  • AI, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning
  • Smart Healthcare, Smart Manufacturing, Intelligent Systems
  • Cyberinfrastructures, Cybersecurity, Privacy and Security
  • High Performance Computing, Storage Systems, System Architecture
  • Resource Management and Performance Optimization
